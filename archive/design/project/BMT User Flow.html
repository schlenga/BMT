section · who we're building for

Four operators. One cockpit that dresses differently.

BMT doesn't serve "SMBs" — it serves people, whose relationship with admin work ranges from mildly annoyed to genuinely allergic. The cockpit should look and feel different for each. Below: four anchor personas, their jobs-to-be-done, and the secret goal — quietly teaching every operator to think like a lean startup.

hidden layer · the secret goal

Every nudge is secretly a lean-startup loop.

The COO never says "hypothesis" or "MVP". But every suggestion it makes is structured as Build → Measure → Learn. Over 12 months, the operator internalizes the loop — and starts thinking about their business with the same rigor a startup founder does.

build · the tiny experiment
"Try 15% off quiet-hour Thursdays for 2 weeks."
measure · the number to watch
"We'll track: Thu chair-use & margin per chair."
learn · the recap, in plain words
"Filled Thu 68% → 92%. Margin up €310/wk. Keep it?"
every experiment has a clear question every result has a plain-word debrief loops compound into operator intuition after 6 months: "you've run 14 experiments"
North star for the copy writer: never teach with jargon. Teach with the shape of the question itself.
